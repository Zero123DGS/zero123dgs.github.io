Zero-1-to-3DGS: a Single Image to 3D Gaussian by Consistent Multi-view Generation

Shenghao Yang * Jianxing Ren * Hongtao Zhang   Zhihao Tang   Mingbo Zhao  

| Paper | Code | HuggingFace Demo | (will coming soon)
 

Abstract

Recent research on generating 3D assets from single-view inputs by diffusion models has attracted great attention. However, existing diffusion models face challenges such as lack of 3D consistency, high computational costs and rough details in generated content. To address these issues, this paper presents a new method, namely, Zero-1-to-3DGS, which is to balance the efficiency and quality for generating a high-quality 3D Gaussians model from a single image within 2 minutes on one GPU. In detail, the proposed work first generates multi-view images that is consistent with input image from diffusion inference model. It then initializes and constrains 3D Gaussians through our improved approach by Visual Hull to speed up and exclude unreasonable Gaussian distributions. Additionally, a Gaussian Repair Module is further designed to improve the quality of the 3D Gaussians model and autonomously correct anomalies. Experimental results show the superiority of the proposed method to other state-of-the-art methods both in effectiveness and efficiency.
